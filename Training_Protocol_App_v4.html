Zone 2 — Mitochondrial Base
▼

Zone 2 trains existing mitochondria to burn lactate as fuel via MCT1 transporters. This only fully engages after ~45 min — the second half of 90 min is where deepest adaptations occur. 2×90 min per week = 180 min, crossing the research threshold for meaningful adaptation.

Why 2×90 min beats 4×45 min

Shorter sessions end before MCT1 and fat oxidation pathways fully engage. Each 90-min session spends 45 min in the deep-adaptation window. Four 45-min sessions spend almost zero time there.

Your benefit: Low HDL (37) + elevated hs-CRP (3.9). Zone 2 is the most evidence-based intervention for both.

Norwegian 4×4 — Mitochondrial Biogenesis
▼

4 intervals of 4 min at 85–95% max HR creates AMPK stress signal → builds entirely new mitochondria. VO2 max is the strongest single predictor of all-cause mortality. Every 3.5 ml/kg/min increase = 13% lower mortality. Zone 2 + 4×4 = matched pair — one builds capacity, the other builds new machinery.

Day Order Science — Why This Sequence
▼

Mon 4×4 after full Sunday rest = hardest session at maximum freshness → better intervals, stronger adaptation signal.
Tue Zone 2 after 4×4 = flush cardio → clears metabolites, accelerates recovery.
Thu Upper A between lower days = genuine zero-leg-load day → breaks the consecutive leg chain.
Lower A→Lower B spacing: 72 hrs with true rest day splitting them. Lower B→Lower A: 4 days. Symmetrical stimulus distribution.
Sun rest immediately after Sat Lower B = complete leg recovery before next week starts.

3-Month Phased Progression

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–3: Foundation Current

4×4 at 80% (~145 bpm). Learn the feel. Add: Tibialis raise, ankle circles, Pallof Press, thoracic rotation. Single-leg balance from Wk 2. Dead hang from Wk 3. Couch stretch Wk 1–3: keep kneeling lunge.

Phase 2 — Weeks 4–5: Build Build

4×4 at 85% (~154 bpm). Add: Farmer's Walk (Thursday end-of-session, NOT Monday). Couch stretch replaces kneeling lunge.

Phase 3 — Weeks 6–8: Accumulate Accumulate

4×4 at 90% (~163 bpm). Add: 5th Cable Lateral Raise set Thursday (only when 4 sets feel easy). NO Reverse Nordic Curl — deleted until patellar tendon ultrasound + physio clearance.

Phase 4 — Weeks 9–12: Peak Peak

4×4 at 90–95% (~163–172 bpm). Nothing new. Progressive overload only. Add weight when all reps feel easy — not on a calendar. DOMS >72h → drop to 2 sets compound.

All warnings in one place — every week

⛔ Cable Fly BEFORE All Pressing
Mon + Thu. Neural deficit requires activation first — always.
⛔ Knee Rehab = Non-Negotiable
All 3 exercises before every lower session (Wed + Sat). LEFT only.
⛔ No Reverse Nordic Curl
Blocked until patellar tendon ultrasound + physio clearance. High risk for your tendinopathy.
⚠️ Off-TRT Recovery Rule
DOMS >72h or declining performance → reduce Fri Zone 2 to 60 min first, then drop compounds to 2 sets.
⚠️ 4×4: Fan Bike — Not Treadmill
Fan bike = zero impact for your left knee during high-intensity intervals.
⚠️ IBS Meal Timing
90 min before Zone 2. 2 hrs before 4×4. Low-fat. Spread protein 4–5 × 25g daily.
⚠️ Zone 2 Talk Test
MUST speak full sentences. If short phrases only → slow down. Guntur heat: use HR monitor, not feel.
⚠️ 4×4 Talk Test (Inverted)
During intervals: if you CAN talk comfortably, push harder. Recovery: if you CAN'T talk, you're not recovering.
⚠️ Monday 10-Min Rest Gap
Between Upper B and 4×4. Walk + 300ml water. Skipping = underperforming intervals.
⚠️ Mon Night: Sleep 8 hrs
80% of 4×4 adaptation happens during deep sleep. Your documented high sleep need = non-negotiable.
⚠️ Farmer's Walk: Thursday Only
Phase 2+ only. End of Thursday session when fresh. NOT after Monday 4×4 when depleted.
⚠️ Goblet Squat Knee Check
Any left knee pain at depth → reduce range to parallel or swap for extra TKE sets.
